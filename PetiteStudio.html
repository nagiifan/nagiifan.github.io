Overview

Petite Studio is a fashion start-up designed for petite women. Half of its customers are Americans of short height, and the others are Chineses. Petite Studio has its online website and launched its SOHO boutique in 2018. Having received a large number of positive feedbacks on its clothing styles and quality from existing customers, now, Petite Studio is eager to increase its customer base and promote its boutique.

Analysis Process

4 P's Of Marketing

In order to understand what kind of product/service Petite Studio provides, through what channels and which group of customers it engages, we used the Marketing Mix to analyze the brand.

Product


  • High quality fabric
  • Modern vintage style
  • Accessories from other fashion brands

Price


  • Price range: $100-$400

Promotion


  • Affliated marketing: mostly on Youtube
  • Referral marketing: Dealmoon/Instagram/Weibo
  • Facebook/Google ads

Place


  • Official website
  • Social media
  • Offline boutique

Target Customer Profile



Petite fashionistas are in the 18- 35 age spectrum and love effortless dressing. They have a great demand for luxurious garments and can afford a high price point. Among these customers, some of them are fashion bloggers; some are Chinese students who are living in the US. They are bold in matching clothes and are willing to share their outfit inspirations on social media platforms.


Competitive Analysis Matrix


We looked into multiple fashion brands which are Petite Studio's direct and indirect competitors, and we find that:

  • Due to its niche market, Petite Studio does have a sharp competitive edge.
  • Competitors listed in the petite-style market like J. Crew and Ann Taylor have petite lines. However, the styles are as same as regular lines.
  • Reformation is the most competitive one for a similar price point and style, while the product quality is inferior to Petite Studio.

Industry & Market Segment Overview

Industry Overview

  • Limited brands have a petite line.
  • Clothing in petite lines is not designed according to the body shape of petite-size people.
  • Petite-size customers often shop in children's wear because they hardly find well-fitting clothing in regular lines.

Market Segment Overview

  • Asian girls studying/working in US.
  • Ethnic Chinese.
  • Petite-sized American.
  • 20-35 year-old girls in China.

Current CRM Tactics

To learn about Petite Studio's CRM tactics, we met with their marketing manager several times, as well as did online research.

  • Selection: Petite-sized girls.
  • Acquisition:

    * Sending product information through email subscription and social platform advertising.
    * Cooperating with fashion bloggers.

  • Retention:

      * Providing instant online-chat service.
      * Organizing customized events.
      * Initiating PETITE CLUB reward program.

  • Extension: Selling and promote accessories from fashion start-ups (e.g. Parisa Wang).

Pros

  • Focusing on the niche market - highly competitive.
  • Intensive exposure on social platforms.
  • Instant customer supports

Cons

  • Limited customers' feedbacks available (excludes collaborating bloggers)
  • Few Dynamic Product Ads.
  • Few content marketing. Blur brand story and image.

CRM improvement

  • Increasing direct interactive conections with customers.
  • Offering coupons when customers refering the brand to friends or sharing shopping experience on social platforms.
  • Developing unique and complete brand stories.

Techniques

Tracking shot enables audiences feel more present in the scene by following the main characters. Viewers would feel like they are also wandering in New York City. They could feel the lovely sunshine and as excited as the characters were in the video.


The location of Petite Studio is critical in this video. We want viewers can have a clear idea of which street is the boutique located. Arc shot can visualize the road map from the audiences' angle, which helps customers to find the store more efficiently.


Dolly shot gives viewers a sense of being in the same space as in the video. This scene is shot from the eye height of a petite girl in order to present an authentic view angle from a customer. I also use the warm white filter to make the in-store atmosphere cozier.
